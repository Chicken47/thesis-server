# PSU Banking — Sector Context

_Last updated: 2026-02-26_

## Growth Outlook

- Gross NPAs of scheduled commercial banks hit a **historic low of 2.15%** as of September 2025 (FinMin, Feb 2026), with RBI data confirming the ratio at **2.1%** by December 2025 — a multi-decade trough.
- Bank provisions fell to a **7-quarter low** as of Q3 FY26, directly improving Return on Assets heading into the December quarter results cycle.
- PSU banks outperformed on profitability through 2025 on a combination of higher net profits and cleaner balance sheets — though Budget 2026 (Feb 1) delivered a negative surprise, with PSU bank stocks falling **up to 7%** post-budget, suggesting the government provided no fresh recapitalisation or meaningful fiscal stimulus directed at the sector.
- A "big-bank move" is reportedly in the works per Economic Times (Feb 3, 2026) — likely referencing consolidation or structural reorganisation — which could reshape competitive dynamics.

- **SBI**: Motilal Oswal's top pick for re-rating alongside PNB (Sep 2025 note); remains the bellwether for PSU bank sentiment.
- **PNB**: Hit a **new 52-week high** in Dec 2025 on Q2 FY26 results momentum; SEBI-driven Nifty Bank index rebalancing flagged PNB as a potential "must-own" alongside Bank of Baroda.
- **Bank of Baroda**: Cited alongside PNB as beneficiary of index inclusion dynamics post-SEBI's Nifty Bank shake-up.

- February 2026 saw a sharp **PSU bank rally vs. private banks**, with the "Sarkari Rally" narrative gaining traction — driven by valuation discount closure and earnings delivery.

## Sector-Specific Risks

- **Budget disappointment**: Zero recapitalisation allocation in Budget 2026 was the key negative trigger — the market had priced in some government support; the absence spooked Dalal Street and drove the 5–7% post-budget correction.
- **Consolidation execution risk**: The ET-flagged "big-bank move" introduces integration uncertainty, management bandwidth concerns, and potential minority shareholder dilution.
- **Credit growth sustainability**: With NPAs at cycle lows, the next leg of risk is whether aggressive lending to maintain growth leads to fresh slippages — especially in unsecured retail and MSME books.
- **Government ownership overhang**: Periodic disinvestment speculation and lack of autonomy in pricing/hiring remain structural discount factors vs. private peers.

## Valuation Context

- PSU banks are trading at a **significant discount to private sector peers** on P/B basis — re-rating thesis is intact per Motilal Oswal but execution-dependent.
- The Feb 2026 rally suggests some compression of the valuation gap, but post-budget correction partially reversed gains — net, the sector remains below historical mean P/B multiples.
- Re-rating catalysts: sustained RoA improvement (provisions at 7-quarter lows are a positive), index rebalancing flows, and any government consolidation announcement.
- De-rating risk: fresh NPA cycle from retail stress or a policy misstep on credit expansion.

## What to Watch (Next Quarter)

1. **Q3 FY26 earnings** (Jan–Mar 2026 reporting season): NIM trajectory and slippage ratios will be the swing factor — provisions are low, so any uptick in fresh NPAs will hit earnings hard.
2. **Details of the "big-bank move"** flagged by Economic Times (Feb 3): Any official announcement on mergers or restructuring will be an immediate re-rating or de-rating catalyst depending on structure.
3. **Nifty Bank index rebalancing**: SEBI-driven reshuffle potentially elevating PNB and BoB weightage — passive flow impact could provide a technical floor for these names in the near term.
